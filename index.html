Tailoring framework

How Coleman can benefit by choosing customer-matched keywords

High demand + high competitionUse precise ad groups for “Camping Tents,” “Camping Equipment,” and “Sale on Tents” so spending is concentrated where customer intent is strongest.
Branded growth signalUse “Coleman Tents” to defend brand traffic, upsell accessories, and retarget shoppers already familiar with Coleman.
Declining or ambiguous termsMonitor “Outdoors Tents” because the -90% YoY change suggests Coleman should test copy before scaling spend.
Niche discoveryUse “Outdoors Accommodations” for content experiments, not major paid spend, because demand is small and bids are unavailable.

Customer feedback loop

Turn searches, clicks, and comments into better marketing decisions

Coleman should combine keyword performance with customer feedback: search terms show what shoppers ask for, while reviews, surveys, service chats, and product ratings explain why they buy or leave. The best campaigns connect both sources.

Measured results
  • Increase paid bids when high-intent keywords produce clicks, conversions, and positive customer comments.
  • Rewrite landing pages when customers search for tents but ask reviews about setup time, family size, or weather protection.
  • Shift budget from broad display ads to paid search when exact-match terms outperform awareness impressions.
